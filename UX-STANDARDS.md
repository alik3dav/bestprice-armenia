# UX Standards

## Principles
Search-first, compare-fast, trust-forward, Armenian-first. Users must quickly find products, compare offers, understand availability, and trust merchant information.

## Navigation and interaction
Header search is primary on public pages. Breadcrumbs are required on category and product detail pages. Filters and sort should persist in URLs where practical. Every control needs hover, focus, active, disabled, and loading states where applicable. Dialogs, drawers, menus, carousels, and forms must be keyboard accessible.

## Conversion
Primary CTAs use consistent labels and placement. Product cards prioritize image, title, price, rating, store count/availability, and secondary compare/favorite actions. Product detail above the fold must expose title, media, best price, availability, merchant action, and trust signals. Sponsored content must be labeled.

## Mobile and forms
Mobile product grids use two columns unless content requires rows. Filters use drawer/bottom-sheet patterns with selected chips and reset/apply actions. Forms require labels, helper/error text, server validation, safe client validation for UX, duplicate-submit prevention, and preservation of safe input after errors.
